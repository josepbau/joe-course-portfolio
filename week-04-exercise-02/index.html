Emmitt Smith | 1996 | NFL

Emmitt Smith is my All-Time favorite Dallas Cowboy! Growing up, I couldn't wait to watch him every Sunday to watch him run that ball down the field and break tackles. Most of all my favorite part was watching him cross that goal line with a fierce run to help his team gain a victory.

3-Time Super Bowl Champion Troy Aikman

Troy Aikman | 1995 | NFL

Troy Aikman was the 1st Overall Pick in the 1989 NFL Draft by the Dallas Cowboys. Troy Aikman played the position of Quarterback and led the team to 3 NFL Super Bowl appearances which resulted in 3 Super Bowl wins. He was the leader of the Dallas Cowboys Dynasty in the 90's. He was a true leader and Captain of this team which established a competitive and winning culture.

The "Playmaker" Michael Irvin

Michael Irvin | 1994 | NFL

Michael Irvin was the Dallas Cowboys First Round Draft pick in 1988 NFL Draft at pick #11. He was called the plamaker due to his ability to making big plays in big games during college and his Pro years. He was one of the three main components for the explosive Dallas Offense and was considered one of the Three Amigos, along with Troy Aikman and Emmitt Smith. Michael Irvin was also inducted intot the Hall of Fame in 2005 and is also a 3-Time Super Bowl Champion.

Deion "Prine-Time" Sanders

Deion Sanders | 1996 | NFL

Deion Sanders, aka Prime-Time, was a free agent that that was signed in 1995 to contribute to the secondary in the Dallas Cowboys defense. Deion is considered to be one fo the gratest defenders ever who played cornerback. Not only was he an outstsanding defender but he also contributed tremendously in Special Teams where he was a Kick/punt returner. This was where he made his name. He was well know for returning punts or kicks for touchedowns with his electric speed and quick moves. He also helped the Dallas Cowboys win a Super Bowl in 1996 against the Pittsburgh Steelers.

Dallas Cowboys Leading Pass-Catcher Jason Witten

Jason Witten | 2010 | NFL

Jason Witten played 17 seasons in the NFL and spent 16 of them with my Dallas Cowboys. He is the team's leading Pass-Catcher and will soon be inducted into the Hall of Fame in 2026. Jason Witten was also my late Father's favorite Dallas Cowboy player. My father was never one to wear jerseys but he would proudly were that #82 jersey with pride. Jason Witten wasn't just a great football, he was also a great human being with his philanthropy work off the field. In 2012 Jason Witten was awarded the Wallter Payton NFL Man of the Year Award for his philanthropic work with his foundation.
